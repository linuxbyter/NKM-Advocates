Debt Recovery & Small Claims

Legal Basis

Small Claims Court Act No. 2 of 2016 · Debts (Summary Recovery) Act (Cap. 42) · Civil Procedure Act (Cap. 21) · Civil Procedure Rules, 2010 · Magistrates' Courts Act · High Court (Organization and Administration) Act, 2016

Unpaid invoices, dishonoured contracts, and outstanding debts disrupt cash flow and undermine business sustainability. Kenyan law provides a structured, escalating set of mechanisms for recovering money owed — from demand letters and negotiated settlements through to court-issued judgments and asset attachment. Our Debt Recovery & Small Claims department structures collections efficiently and enforces judgments effectively.

What We Do

  • Demand Letters — The first step in any debt recovery action is a formal demand letter, which sets out the amount owed, the basis of the claim, and a reasonable deadline for payment. A well-drafted demand letter can trigger settlement without litigation, and it frames any subsequent court proceedings by establishing that the creditor pursued payment reasonably before filing.
  • Small Claims Court (SCC) — The Small Claims Court, established under the Small Claims Court Act No. 2 of 2016, handles civil and commercial claims up to KES 1,000,000. It is designed for speed and accessibility: the Act requires the Adjudicator to deliver a decision within 60 days of filing, and many Nairobi SCC cases conclude within 53 working days — dramatically faster than ordinary civil litigation. We prepare and file statements of claim, organise documentary evidence, and represent clients at hearings.
  • Magistrates' Court Recovery — For claims exceeding the SCC threshold, we file suit in the Magistrates' Court, where the monetary jurisdiction is set out in the Magistrates' Courts Act. We advise on summary judgment applications where the debtor has made written admissions of the debt, and on applications for judgment in default of appearance where the debtor fails to respond.
  • Judgment Enforcement — A judgment you cannot enforce is a piece of paper. After obtaining judgment, we pursue enforcement through attachment of the debtor's movable property, garnishee orders against banks or employers holding funds belonging to the debtor, and, where necessary, attachment of immovable property. We assess the debtor's known assets before filing to advise on the most effective enforcement route.
  • Commercial Debt Recovery — For businesses managing multiple outstanding receivables, we provide structured recovery programmes, including debtor profiling, escalation protocols, and batch filing of related claims.
