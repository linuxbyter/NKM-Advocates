Mediation, Arbitration & ADR

Legal Basis

Arbitration Act No. 4 of 1995 (as amended 2009) · Constitution of Kenya 2010, Article 159(2)(c) · Civil Procedure Act (Cap. 21) · Civil Procedure (Court-Annexed Mediation) Rules, 2022 · Nairobi Centre for International Arbitration (NCIA) Rules, 2015 · New York Convention on the Recognition and Enforcement of Foreign Arbitral Awards (Kenya is a signatory)

Not every dispute needs a courtroom. Article 159(2)(c) of the Constitution of Kenya expressly mandates that courts promote alternative dispute resolution, including reconciliation, mediation, arbitration, and traditional dispute resolution mechanisms. Our ADR department provides structured, confidential resolution of commercial and family disputes that preserves relationships, reduces cost, and delivers outcomes far faster than litigation.

What We Do

  • Mediation — Mediation is a confidential, voluntary process in which a neutral mediator facilitates dialogue between disputing parties to reach a mutually acceptable agreement. The mediator does not impose a decision. Once the parties sign a settlement agreement, it is legally binding under the Civil Procedure (Court-Annexed Mediation) Rules 2022. Mediation is widely used in commercial, contractual, and family disputes and is frequently ordered by the High Court's Commercial and Family Divisions as a pre-trial step.
  • Arbitration — Arbitration is a more formal ADR process governed by the Arbitration Act, 1995. A neutral arbitrator or panel hears both sides and delivers a legally binding arbitral award, which can be enforced in the High Court and, under the New York Convention, in foreign jurisdictions. The Arbitration Act is modelled on the UNCITRAL Model Law and restricts court intervention in arbitral proceedings (Section 10), preserving party autonomy. We draft arbitration clauses for inclusion in commercial contracts and represent clients in arbitral proceedings before the NCIA and other institutions.
  • ADR Clause Drafting — We draft clear, unambiguous dispute resolution clauses for commercial contracts specifying whether disputes are to be referred to mediation, arbitration, or a tiered process, the governing rules, the seat, and the language of proceedings. Kenyan courts enforce these clauses provided they are sufficiently clear.
  • Accredited Mediation Services — Our Managing Partner Agnes Nyawira is an accredited mediator. We provide mediation services for commercial disputes, landlord-tenant disputes, partnership and shareholder disagreements, and family matters including estate distribution disputes.
  • Settlement Agreements — Where parties have reached a settlement through negotiation, we document the agreement in a legally enforceable format, advise on its terms, and register it with the court where applicable.
