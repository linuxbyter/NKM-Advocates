Intellectual Property

Legal Basis

Trade Marks Act (Cap. 506) · Industrial Property Act No. 3 of 2001 · Copyright Act (Cap. 130) · Anti-Counterfeit Act No. 13 of 2008 · Kenya Industrial Property Institute (KIPI) · Kenya Copyright Board (KECOBO) · Madrid Agreement and Madrid Protocol (Kenya is a member)

Your business name, logo, product design, software, creative content, and proprietary processes are assets. In Kenya's increasingly competitive market, failing to register and protect these assets creates risk — from copycat competitors to infringement claims that arise after investment has already been made. Our Intellectual Property department protects what you create, under the laws that govern it.

What We Do

  • Trademark Registration — A trademark is registered with the Kenya Industrial Property Institute (KIPI) under the Trade Marks Act (Cap. 506). Before filing, we conduct a clearance search (Form TM27) to check for conflicting marks. Registration grants exclusive rights for 10 years, renewable indefinitely in 10-year periods. Kenya is a member of the Madrid Protocol, which enables international trademark protection through a single WIPO application — we advise on and file Madrid system applications for clients seeking multi-country protection.
  • Trademark Monitoring & Enforcement — Registration is only the first step. We monitor the KIPI Industrial Property Journal during the 60-day opposition window and file oppositions where confusingly similar marks are published. We pursue infringers through cease-and-desist notices, KIPI dispute procedures, and where necessary, High Court litigation.
  • Copyright Protection — Copyright in Kenya is automatic upon creation of an original work under the Copyright Act (Cap. 130) and is administered by the Kenya Copyright Board (KECOBO). Protection lasts for the author's lifetime plus 50 years. We advise on copyright ownership, licensing, assignment, and enforcement — including digital copyright infringement, which is increasingly common for Kenyan content creators, publishers, and software developers.
  • Patents & Industrial Designs — Patents and industrial designs are registered with KIPI under the Industrial Property Act, 2001. We advise inventors and manufacturers on patentability, file patent applications, and protect product designs from copying.
  • IP Licensing & Assignment — We draft licensing agreements for the use of trademarks, copyright works, and patents, and advise on IP assignments as part of business acquisitions and franchise arrangements.
  • Brand Protection for SMEs — Many Kenyan SMEs unknowingly operate without trademark protection and discover the risk only when a competitor copies their brand. We offer an accessible brand audit and registration service designed specifically for growing businesses.
