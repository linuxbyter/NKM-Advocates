Data Protection

Legal Basis

Data Protection Act No. 24 of 2019 · Data Protection (General) Regulations, 2021 · Data Protection (Registration of Data Controllers and Data Processors) Regulations, 2021 · Data Protection (Complaints Handling Procedure and Enforcement) Regulations, 2021 · Constitution of Kenya 2010, Article 31(c) and (d) · Office of the Data Protection Commissioner (ODPC)

Kenya's Data Protection Act, 2019 (DPA) is the country's first comprehensive privacy law, modelled on the EU's GDPR and enacted to give effect to Article 31 of the Constitution. The Office of the Data Protection Commissioner (ODPC) is actively enforcing the Act — issuing penalty notices of up to KES 5 million, conducting compliance audits, and processing thousands of data subject complaints. Compliance is not aspirational; it is a legal obligation for every business handling personal data.

What We Do

  • ODPC Registration — The DPA prohibits any person from acting as a data controller or data processor without being registered with the Data Commissioner. Mandatory registration thresholds include businesses with annual turnover above KES 5 million, those with more than 10 employees, those processing sensitive personal data, those processing data of 10,000 or more data subjects per year, and all entities in regulated sectors including financial services, healthcare, education, and telecommunications. We manage the registration process and monitor renewal obligations.
  • Data Protection Compliance Audits — We conduct structured compliance assessments covering: data inventory and mapping of all personal data processed; identification of the lawful basis for each processing activity (consent, contract, legal obligation, legitimate interest); review of privacy notices, data sharing arrangements, and processor contracts; and assessment of security measures, breach notification procedures, and data subject rights management.
  • Privacy Notices & Consent Frameworks — Every organization that collects personal data must provide a clear, accessible privacy notice. Consent under the DPA must be freely given, specific, informed, and unambiguous. The ODPC has fined organisations for processing personal data without a valid lawful basis, including fining a restaurant for posting an employee photograph on social media without consent. We draft compliant privacy notices and consent forms and advise on their implementation.
  • Data Subject Rights Management — The DPA grants data subjects the rights to access, rectification, erasure, objection, and data portability. Organisations must respond within the statutory timeframe. The ODPC has processed over 9,000 data protection complaints, demonstrating that data subjects in Kenya actively exercise these rights. We design internal procedures for handling these requests and advise on complex erasure and objection scenarios.
  • Cross-Border Data Transfers — Transferring personal data outside Kenya requires either adequate protections in the receiving country, explicit consent, contractual safeguards (Standard Contractual Clauses), or Binding Corporate Rules for intra-group transfers. This directly affects any business using foreign-hosted cloud services, payment processors, HR platforms, or analytics providers. We advise on compliant transfer mechanisms.
  • Data Protection Impact Assessments (DPIAs) — High-risk processing activities require a DPIA before they begin. We conduct DPIAs for new products, systems, or processes that involve significant data processing — identifying risks and the measures required to mitigate them.
  • Data Protection Officer (DPO) Services — Certain categories of organisation are required to appoint a Data Protection Officer. We provide outsourced DPO services for organisations that need this function without the cost of a full-time internal appointment.
