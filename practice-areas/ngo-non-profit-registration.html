NGO & Non-Profit Registration

Legal Basis

Public Benefit Organizations Act, 2013 (operationalised May 2024) · PBO Regulations, Legal Notice No. 43 of 2026 · Societies Act (Cap. 108) · Companies Act, 2015 (for Companies Limited by Guarantee) · Trustees Act (Cap. 167) · Income Tax Act (Cap. 470) — Income Tax (Charitable Organizations and Donations) Exemption Rules, 2024

Kenya's non-profit sector is governed by a significantly updated legal framework. The Public Benefit Organizations (PBO) Act, 2013, operationalised in May 2024 and supported by detailed PBO Regulations published in March 2026, has replaced the old NGO Coordination Act framework. Every organisation previously registered as an NGO must now operate under the new regime. We guide founders, diaspora philanthropists, and international organisations through registration, compliance, and governance under the current law.

What We Do

  • Entity Selection & Structuring — The appropriate legal vehicle for a non-profit depends on its purpose, governance model, and funding sources. We advise on the four main options: Public Benefit Organization (PBO) registered under the PBO Act 2013; Society registered under the Societies Act; Company Limited by Guarantee registered under the Companies Act, 2015; and Charitable Trust under the Trustees Act (Cap. 167). Each carries distinct governance requirements, tax implications, and regulatory obligations.
  • PBO Registration — We handle the full registration process with the Public Benefit Organizations Regulatory Authority, including constitution drafting, founders' documentation, submission, and follow-up. Registration is mandatory — organisations that claim PBO status without registration commit a criminal offence under the PBO Act. International organisations intending to operate in Kenya must ensure that at least one-third of their directors are Kenyan citizens residing in Kenya and maintain a physical office in Kenya.
  • KRA Tax Exemption — Registered PBOs may apply for income tax exemption under the Income Tax (Charitable Organizations and Donations) Exemption Rules, 2024. Qualification requires demonstrating that the organisation is constituted solely for public benefit purposes and that no income or assets are distributed to members. We prepare and file KRA exemption applications and advise on maintaining compliance with the exemption conditions.
  • Governance & Constitution Drafting — The PBO Act places significant emphasis on internal governance. We draft constitutions that meet regulatory requirements, establish clear separation between governance and management, and protect the organization's mission from founder-syndrome disputes — a leading cause of non-profit dissolution in Kenya.
  • Annual Compliance & Reporting — PBOs have mandatory annual reporting obligations under the PBO Act. Non-compliance has resulted in the PBORA issuing notices against over 2,800 organisations for deregistration. We manage ongoing compliance filings on behalf of our non-profit clients.
