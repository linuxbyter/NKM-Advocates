Family Law

Legal Basis

Law of Succession Act (Cap. 160) · Marriage Act, 2014 · Matrimonial Property Act, 2013 · Children Act, 2022 · Powers of Attorney Act (Cap. 531) · Constitution of Kenya 2010, Article 45 · Judicature Act (Cap. 8) — applicability of customary and religious law

Family legal matters are among the most personal a client will ever face. Distance — particularly when a client is based abroad — can make these matters more urgent and more complex. Our Family Law department handles succession, estate administration, matrimonial property, custody, and powers of attorney with the sensitivity these matters require and the cross-border understanding our diaspora clients need.

What We Do

  • Wills & Testamentary Planning — Every person of sound mind who is not a minor may make a will under the Law of Succession Act (Cap. 160). A written will must be signed by the testator in the presence of at least two witnesses who must also sign. We draft wills that are clear, legally sound, and consistent with the testator's intentions — including provisions for minor children, property held in multiple jurisdictions, and business succession. Where a client holds assets in both Kenya and a foreign country, we advise on mirror will strategies to avoid conflicts between jurisdictions.
  • Grant of Probate & Letters of Administration — After a death, the deceased's estate cannot be distributed without a grant of representation from the High Court (or Magistrates' Court for smaller estates). We apply for grants of probate (where there is a will) and letters of administration (where there is no will), and manage the estate administration process through to distribution to beneficiaries. Under Section 4 of the Law of Succession Act, succession to immovable property in Kenya is always governed by Kenyan law regardless of the deceased's domicile.
  • Intestate Succession — Where a person dies without a will, the Law of Succession Act sets out the default distribution rules in Sections 35 to 40, prioritising the surviving spouse and children. We advise families on these rules, identify all assets forming the estate, and manage the administration process.
  • Power of Attorney — A Power of Attorney (PoA) under the Powers of Attorney Act (Cap. 531) authorises a Kenya-resident person to act on your behalf in legal and financial matters. We draft PoAs tailored to specific purposes — land transactions, business management, bank account operations, or general management of Kenya affairs — and advise on the scope and limitations of the authority granted. For clients abroad, we prepare the PoA document for execution and, where required, advise on notarisation and consular legalisation procedures.
  • Matrimonial Property & Divorce — The Matrimonial Property Act, 2013 governs the ownership and division of marital property. Property acquired during marriage through joint effort is presumed jointly owned. We advise on matrimonial property rights, represent clients in divorce proceedings, and negotiate property settlement agreements.
  • Child Custody & Guardianship — The Children Act, 2022 governs all matters relating to children in Kenya. In custody matters, the welfare of the child is the paramount consideration. We represent parents in custody disputes, advise on parental responsibility, and assist in applying for guardianship orders for orphaned or abandoned children.

Diaspora Advantage

  • → Prepare and execute a Kenya Power of Attorney while you are abroad, enabling a trusted representative to manage your property, business, or family affairs in Kenya
  • → Administer a deceased relative's estate in Kenya entirely remotely — from grant of representation through to final distribution — with regular updates via WhatsApp or email
  • → Draft a Kenya-specific will that addresses your Kenyan assets, including land, bank accounts, and business interests, in a form that will be recognised and administered by Kenyan courts
  • → Reseal a foreign grant of probate or letters of administration in the Kenyan High Court under Section 79 of the Law of Succession Act, which gives foreign grants the same force and effect as Kenyan grants
  • → Advise on cross-border inheritance: where a Kenyan diaspora client holds assets in both Kenya and the UK, US, or Gulf, we identify which law governs each asset class and coordinate with foreign lawyers where needed
  • → Represent your interests in succession disputes among Kenya-resident family members while you remain abroad
