Real Estate

Legal Basis

Land Registration Act, 2012 · Land Act No. 6 of 2012 · National Land Commission Act, 2012 · Land Control Act (Cap. 302) · Stamp Duty Act (Cap. 480) · Environment and Land Court Act, 2011 · Ardhisasa Platform (Ministry of Lands)

Land in Kenya is both its most valuable asset class and one of its most litigated. Title fraud, double allocations, and forged succession documents cost Kenyan buyers billions of shillings annually — and diaspora investors, unable to visit properties in person, are disproportionately targeted. Our Real Estate department provides rigorous, on-the-ground due diligence and transactional support grounded in the current legal framework.

What We Do

  • Title Verification & Due Diligence — Under the Land Registration Act, 2012, a title deed is the primary proof of ownership, but its authenticity must always be verified against official registry records. We conduct official searches through the Ardhisasa platform and physically visit the lands registry to confirm the registered owner, check for encumbrances (mortgages, caveats, cautions), and verify that the title is genuine and unencumbered. Section 26 of the Land Registration Act provides that fraud renders a title voidable — a buyer who fails due diligence has limited legal recourse.
  • Sale Agreements & Transfer of Title — We draft and negotiate sale agreements, lodge caveats to protect buyers after signing, conduct stamp duty assessment and payment, and handle the full title transfer process at the relevant lands registry. The title transfer process in Kenya typically takes 45 to 90 days, involving valuation, stamp duty clearance, land rent clearance, and formal registration.
  • Land Control Board Consent — The Land Control Act (Cap. 302) requires prior consent of the Land Control Board for any sale, transfer, lease, or other dealing in agricultural land. Transactions conducted without the required consent are void. We identify when consent is required and obtain it as part of the transaction.
  • Lease Agreements — We draft commercial and residential lease agreements, advise landlords and tenants on their respective rights and obligations, and review existing leases before signature.
  • Land Dispute Resolution — Disputes relating to land are heard by the Environment and Land Court, which has exclusive jurisdiction in such matters. Over 50% of civil cases in the Kenyan courts involve land disputes. We advise on available legal remedies, including injunctions, caveats, and claims for revocation of fraudulently obtained titles under the National Land Commission Act, 2012.
  • Sectional Properties & Off-Plan Purchases — We advise buyers on off-plan and sectional property purchases, including reviewing developer agreements, verifying development approvals, and protecting client deposits.
